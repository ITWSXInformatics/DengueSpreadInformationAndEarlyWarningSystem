Mosquitos while a pest are far more terrifying than most people realize. Not only do these insects suck blood from victims but, they carry with them a wide range of diseases. One of these diseases, Dengue, became rampant in 2007 and 2008 in Iquitos, Peru and San Juan, Puerto Rico. As a result, the team decided to create an Information System to help visualize and analyze the factors in these two cities.


Factors impacting the spread of Dengue


There are many factors that play a role in determining how many individuals become infected with Dengue. The primary factor though is the mosquito population and the factors that promote the an increase in the population of the mosquitos in Dengue. The factors include the following:

  1. Standing bodies of water
  2. The temperature
  3. Cloud coverage
  4. Population density
