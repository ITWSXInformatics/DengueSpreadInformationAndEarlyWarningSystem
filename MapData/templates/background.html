Dengue Spread Information System (DSIS)

Dengue Spread Information System is a web application developed to highlight the spread of Dengue across Iquitos, Peru and San Juan, Puerto Rico from 1990 to 2013. The application includes an interactive map and several static risk factor plots that might explain the spread of Dengue across a region.

On the similar lines, one of these mosquito-borne diseases, Dengue, became rampant in 2007 and 2008 in Iquitos, Peru and San Juan, Puerto Rico.

Thus, to understand the spread of Dengue and associated risk factors, the Dengue Spread Information System (DSIS) was created as a system to help visualize and analyze several Dengue risk factors in these two cities. The aim is to enable researchers and policymakers alike to understand Dengue spread and develop policies aroung them.

Risk Factors impacting the spread of Dengue

There are many factors that play a role in determining how many individuals become infected with Dengue. The primary factor though is the mosquito population and the factors that promote the an increase in the population of the mosquitos in Dengue. The factors include the following (Source: WHO, CDC and IAMAT):

  1. Standing bodies of water
  2. The temperature
  3. Cloud coverage
  4. Population density
